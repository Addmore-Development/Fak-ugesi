Who we are

Fak'ugesi African Digital & Innovation Festival

Fak'ugesi — a Zulu word meaning "plug in" or "connect" — has been the heartbeat of African digital culture since 2013. For a decade, it has gathered artists, technologists, entrepreneurs, and dreamers in Johannesburg to imagine, build, and celebrate Africa's digital future.

The 2026 edition marks our 10th anniversary — a milestone we celebrate not with nostalgia, but with ambition. This year's festival is our most expansive yet: 10 days, 200+ sessions, 80 countries represented, and a flagship immersive exhibition at the iconic Turbine Hall.

We believe that Africa's digital future is not something that will happen to us — it is something we will build ourselves. Fak'ugesi is a space to showcase that work, to challenge, to learn, to fail, to celebrate, and to connect.

Fak'ugesi is produced by The Innovation Hub, Gauteng's premier innovation agency, in partnership with cultural and technology institutions across the continent. In 2026, we ask: What does Africa's digital decade look like?
