"Immersive Africa is not just an exhibition — it is a portal. A space where the ancestors speak in code, and the future looks like home."
— Zanele Mokoena, Curator, Immersive Africa 2026
Immersive Africa is the flagship exhibition of Fak'ugesi Festival — a curated gathering of boundary-pushing immersive works from African artists working in VR, augmented reality, projection mapping, installation art, and interactive digital media.
Held in the iconic Turbine Hall in Newtown, Johannesburg, the 2026 edition brings together 80+ works from 40+ artists across 25 African nations.

Featured Artist
🌌
2026 Featured Artist
Athanasius Johnson
🇿🇦 Johannesburg, South Africa
Athanasius Johnson is a Johannesburg-based XR director and digital artist whose work sits at the intersection of African oral tradition and immersive technology. His studio, AfriVerse, has produced landmark works exhibited from Davos to the Venice Biennale.
His Fak'ugesi 2025 work, AfriVerse: Ancestral Futures, won the Immersive Media Award and toured 12 cities across Africa and Europe. For 2026, Johnson debuts Ancestral Frequencies — his most ambitious work yet.
Featured Work: Ancestral Frequencies
A 20-minute fully immersive VR dome experience for up to 30 simultaneous viewers, blending spatial audio recordings from 8 African nations with generative visuals derived from traditional textile patterns.
Venue
The Turbine Hall
One of Johannesburg's most iconic spaces, the Turbine Hall is a former coal-fired power station turned cultural powerhouse. Its vast industrial interiors — 4,500 square metres of raw steel, concrete and glass — provide the perfect canvas for the large-scale immersive works of Immersive Africa.
